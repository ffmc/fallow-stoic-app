Anxiety

Worry about what might happen.

REFLECT

Name the thing you are anxious about. Now separate the event from your judgment of it: what is actually happening, and what is the story you are telling yourself on top of it? Set the story down for a moment and ask what, if anything, is yours to do right now.

From the Stoics

"Men are disturbed not by things, but by the views which they take of things."

Epictetus · Enchiridion, 5

"If thou art pained by any external thing, it is not this thing that disturbs thee, but thy own judgment about it. And it is in thy power to wipe out this judgment now."

Marcus Aurelius · Meditations, 8.47

"To-day I have got out of all trouble, or rather I have cast out all trouble, for it was not outside, but within and in my opinions."

Marcus Aurelius · Meditations, 9.13

"There are things which are within our power, and there are things which are beyond our power."

Epictetus · Enchiridion, 1
